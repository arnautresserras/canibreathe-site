Privacy Policy

Effective date: 1 April 2025  ·  Last updated: 1 April 2025

Short version: canIbreathe does not collect, store, or share any personal information on our servers. Your location and allergy profile are stored only on your device.

1. Who we are

canIbreathe is a personal project developed by Arnau Tresserras. If you have any questions about this policy, please contact arnau@tresserras.dev.

2. What data the app uses

canIbreathe stores the following information locally on your device only, using your device's private storage. This data never leaves your device and is never transmitted to our servers:

  • Location — the city or pollen monitoring station you choose manually within the app. The app does not access your device's GPS or precise location.
  • Allergen preferences — the list of pollen types and allergens you select.
  • Notification settings — whether daily alerts are enabled, the alert time, and the alert threshold.
  • Language preference — the display language you choose in Settings.

You can reset all of this data at any time via Settings → Reset onboarding.

3. Third-party services

To provide pollen forecasts the app communicates with the following external services. Each request is made directly from your device.

Open-Meteo (open-meteo.com)
Used to fetch pollen forecasts for locations outside the PIA network. Your selected coordinates (latitude and longitude) are sent as part of the API request. Open-Meteo does not require an account and does not track individual users. See their terms.

PIA — Aerobiological Information Programme (aerobiologia.cat)
Used to fetch detailed station-based pollen data for Catalonia and the Balearic Islands. Requests are routed through a Cloudflare Worker proxy to work around CORS restrictions; the proxy forwards the request and does not log personal information. Data is provided under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0.

Nominatim / OpenStreetMap (nominatim.org)
Used for city search when setting your location. The text you type in the location search box is sent to Nominatim. See the OpenStreetMap Foundation privacy policy.

Sentry (sentry.io)
Used for crash reporting in production builds. If the app crashes, an anonymised error report is sent automatically. Reports may include the device model, OS version, and a stack trace. No names, email addresses, or account details are included. See Sentry's privacy policy.

PostHog (posthog.com)
Used for anonymous usage analytics (e.g. which features are used, which languages are selected). Events are captured automatically and do not include any personally identifiable information. Data is hosted on PostHog's EU servers. See PostHog's privacy policy.

4. Notifications

If you enable daily alerts, the app schedules a local notification on your device at the time you choose. Notifications are generated locally — no notification server or push service is used. You can disable notifications at any time in the app's Settings or in your device's system settings.

5. Children's privacy

canIbreathe is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.

6. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

7. Contact

Questions or requests regarding your data can be sent to arnau@tresserras.dev.
